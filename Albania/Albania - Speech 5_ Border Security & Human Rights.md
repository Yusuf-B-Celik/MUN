Honorable Chair, Distinguished Delegates,  
The delegation of Albania wishes to address the third question of our study guide: how to balance strong border security with the protection of human rights.  
For Albania, security and human rights are not opposites. In fact, they must go hand-in-hand. We cannot have true security if we ignore the fundamental rights of the people fleeing for their lives.  
As a nation in the Western Balkans, Albania knows that unstable borders lead to security risks, but closed borders can lead to human tragedies. Therefore, NATO must support member states in establishing secure and humane border management.  
Albania proposes that NATO should assist frontline states by providing logistical support and monitoring technology. This will help border authorities manage the high volume of refugees in an orderly way.  
Furthermore, while we must protect our borders against security threats and illegal human trafficking, we must ensure that genuine asylum seekers are treated with dignity and have access to legal protection.  
Let us prove to the world that NATO can protect its borders without losing its humanity.  
Thank you, and I yield my time back to the Chair.
