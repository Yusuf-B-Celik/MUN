Honorable Chair, Distinguished Delegates,  
As we reach the final stages of our debate, the delegation of Albania believes we must look beyond the immediate crisis. Protecting the victims of this war is not just about keeping them alive today; it is about securing their tomorrow.  
A comprehensive and lasting peace is only possible if we plan for the future. Even when the weapons fall silent, the invisible scars of war will remain.  
Therefore, Albania calls on the Council to focus on two long-term areas in our final resolution:  
First, we must support humanitarian demining. Millions of landmines are currently active in Ukrainian fields, posing a daily threat to children and returning families. NATO must share its expertise and technology to make these lands safe again.  
Second, we must prioritize psychological and social support. The trauma experienced by displaced children and families will affect generations. NATO, in cooperation with civilian organizations, should support mental health programs to help victims rebuild their lives.  
Distinguished delegates, let our final resolution reflect not just our military strength, but our collective humanity. Let us vote for a future where Ukrainian children can walk safely on their streets and sleep without fear.  
Thank you, and I yield my time back to the Chair.
