Honorable Chair, Distinguished Delegates,  
The delegation of Albania wishes to direct the Council’s attention to the most heartbreaking aspect of the Russia-Ukraine war: the displacement of millions of innocent civilians.  
According to our study guide, over 4.3 million Ukrainians have fled to the European Union, creating a massive population crisis and deep psychological trauma. As a nation in the Western Balkans that historically understands the pain of regional conflict and displacement, Albania believes we cannot remain passive.  
To answer the first and fourth questions of our committee, Albania proposes two concrete actions:  
First, we must ensure safe shelter by strictly upholding International Humanitarian Law. Targeting civilian infrastructure, schools, and hospitals is a war crime, and those responsible must be held accountable.  
Second, we must improve the social adaptation of refugees. Albania supports closer cooperation between NATO, the European Union, and humanitarian organizations to fund education, healthcare, and job training programs. Integration is not just about giving refugees a place to sleep; it is about giving them their dignity back.  
Let us work together to build a strong, coordinated framework that protects the human faces of this tragedy.  
Thank you, and I yield my time back to the Chair.
