Honorable Chair, Distinguished Delegates,  
The delegation of Albania believes that while providing food, medicine, and shelter is extremely important, we must also focus on preventing the damage before it happens.  
The current situation in Ukraine is devastating. Day after day, Russian missiles, guided bombs, and drones target civilian neighborhoods, power grids, and water systems. As long as the skies remain unprotected, no amount of humanitarian aid will be enough to save lives. We cannot simply rebuild hospitals only to see them bombed again.  
Therefore, Albania argues that the most effective way to protect civilians is to strengthen Ukraine’s air defense capabilities.  
We urge NATO member states to coordinate and accelerate the delivery of modern air defense systems. By securing Ukraine’s airspace, we can protect schools, keep energy grids running, and allow families to stay safely in their homes rather than fleeing as refugees.  
Humanitarian aid treats the symptoms of this war, but air defense prevents the tragedy. Let us close the skies to open a path to safety.  
Thank you, and I yield my time back to the Chair.
